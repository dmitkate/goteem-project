The freezing process is, in fact, the process of turning a liquid mixture into ice cream. This is achieved with the help of continuous freezers, in which the mixture is whipped with air and frozen to a temperature of -4 ° C.

Immediately after freezing, ice cream is packaged and hardened (shock frozen in an air stream at a temperature of -25 to -37 °C). Hardened ice cream is packed in individual or group packaging. Packaged ice cream is placed in long-term storage warehouses, from where it is further shipped to the distribution network.
